About

Currently, more than half of the food produced in Canada is lost or wasted during processing and manufacturing of food. This amounts to 4.82 million tonnes of food, valued at about $21 billion. Another 2.38 million tonnes are wasted by consumers in Canada. For some perspective, this is the same weight as about 1.2 million fully grown African elephants.

This amount of food waste is also enough to feed every Canadian for five months.

So why do millions of Canadians still go hungry?

Food for Thought aims to incentivize grocery stores to donate their excess food, rather than to throw it away to the landfill. To do so, we created a web application that displays to consumers the amount of food that was diverted by local grocery stores. We will partner with Food Banks, which will use our software to input the amounts of food they received. In addition, interested grocery stores can sign up through our website and be connected to a nearby Food Bank or charity. Finally, we created a leaderboard that visualizes the amount of food donated by each grocery store.

In the future, we hope to expand as a management tool for Food Banks and charities for their input and output of donated goods.
